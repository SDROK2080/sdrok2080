Study Tips & Guidance

Practical advice for excelling in the MBS 2nd Semester from your subject lecturer.

📚 Read the Syllabus First
Download the official TU MBS syllabus and map every topic before classes begin. Know what's examinable.
📊 Practice with SPSS
Business Research Methods requires SPSS proficiency. Practice regression and ANOVA weekly using real datasets.
📝 APA 7th Edition
All assignments must follow APA 7th edition formatting. Master in-text citations and reference lists early.
🔁 Revise Daily
Revise the day's lecture notes the same evening. A 15-minute recap prevents 3 hours of re-reading before exams.
📰 Follow Business News
Real examples strengthen exam answers. Read financial news daily to apply theory to Nepali business contexts.
👥 Form Study Groups
Collaborative learning helps across all six subjects. Teaching peers is the best test of your own understanding.

MGT 512 · 3 Credit Hours

Organizational Behavior

Core CourseFull Marks: 100Pass: 40

This course focuses on understanding human behavior within organizational settings at individual, group, and organizational levels.

Key Topics:

  • Foundations of individual behavior & perception
  • Motivation theories (Maslow, Herzberg, Expectancy)
  • Group dynamics, team building, conflict resolution
  • Leadership theories and styles
  • Organizational culture and change management
  • Communication and decision making

Recommended Textbook: Robbins, S.P. & Judge, T.A. — Organizational Behavior (Pearson)

MGT 513 · 3 Credit Hours

Financial Management

Core CourseFull Marks: 100Pass: 40

Covers theory and practice of corporate finance, investment decisions, and financial planning within Nepali and international business contexts.

Key Topics:

  • Time value of money and financial mathematics
  • Capital budgeting (NPV, IRR, Payback Period)
  • Cost of capital and capital structure
  • Dividend policy and retained earnings
  • Working capital management
  • Leverage analysis (operating & financial)

Recommended Textbook: Pandey, I.M. — Financial Management (Vikas Publishing)

MGT 514 · 3 Credit Hours

Marketing Management

Core CourseFull Marks: 100Pass: 40

A comprehensive study of marketing strategy, consumer behavior analysis, and integrated marketing communications.

Key Topics:

  • Marketing environment and SWOT analysis
  • Consumer behavior and buying decision process
  • Market segmentation, targeting & positioning (STP)
  • Product, Price, Place, Promotion (4Ps)
  • Digital marketing and social media strategy
  • Marketing research and analytics

Recommended Textbook: Kotler, P. & Keller, K.L. — Marketing Management (Pearson)

MGT 515 · 3 Credit Hours

Production & Operations Management

Core CourseFull Marks: 100Pass: 40

Covers the planning, design, and control of production and service operations, including quantitative decision tools and supply chain management.

Key Topics:

  • Production planning and scheduling
  • Facility location and layout design
  • Inventory management (EOQ, JIT, ABC analysis)
  • Quality management (TQM, Six Sigma)
  • Supply chain and logistics management
  • Operations Research tools (LP, PERT/CPM)

Recommended Textbook: Chase, Aquilano & Jacobs — Operations Management for Competitive Advantage (McGraw-Hill)

MGT 516 · 3 Credit Hours

Business Research Methods

Core CourseFull Marks: 100Pass: 40

Equips students with quantitative and qualitative research skills, SPSS application, and APA 7th edition academic writing standards.

Key Topics:

  • Research design: exploratory, descriptive, causal
  • Sampling techniques and sample size determination
  • Questionnaire design and data collection
  • SPSS: regression, ANOVA, correlation, factor analysis
  • APA 7th edition citation and referencing
  • Research report writing and presentation

Recommended Textbook: Kothari, C.R. — Research Methodology: Methods and Techniques (New Age International)

MGT 517 · 3 Credit Hours

Elective / Specialization

ElectiveFull Marks: 100Pass: 40

Students choose one specialization track based on their career focus. The elective deepens expertise in a functional area of business.

Available Tracks:

  • 🏦 Finance — Investment analysis, portfolio management, derivatives
  • 📣 Marketing — Brand management, consumer psychology, retail strategy
  • 👥 Human Resource Management — Talent management, HRD, labor relations
  • 🏛️ Banking & Insurance — NRB regulations, insurance products, credit risk

Consult your campus coordinator or Rayamajhi Sir to confirm which elective is offered in your semester.
