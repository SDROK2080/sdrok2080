About This Guide

The Integrated Student Capacity Development Training Model (ISCDTM–2026) is a SMART training framework designed to build well-rounded, future-ready students across community and institutional schools. It brings digital literacy, life skills, civic responsibility, and community engagement into a single, structured municipality-wide program.

This guide equips municipal education offices, school leaders, and trainers with a ready-to-implement roadmap: target groups, duration, modules, methodology, assessment tools, expected outcomes, and partnership structures.

Assessment Strategy

Assessment is continuous and multi-format, ensuring learning is measured — the “M” in SMART — at every stage of the training.

Pre-training assessmentEstablishes each student's baseline knowledge and skill level before training begins.
Daily reflectionShort end-of-day feedback capturing learning, challenges, and confidence growth.
Group presentationsTeams present findings or solutions to reinforce communication and teamwork skills.
Practical assignmentsHands-on tasks that apply each module's concepts to real situations.
Community project evaluationAssessment of the final group project's relevance, execution, and community impact.
Post-training assessmentMeasures knowledge and skill gained against the pre-training baseline.
Certificate of completionFormal recognition awarded to students who meet attendance and performance criteria.

Expected Outcomes

After completing the training, students will:

  • ✓Develop digital and AI literacy.
  • ✓Strengthen communication and leadership skills.
  • ✓Improve critical thinking and creativity.
  • ✓Understand career and entrepreneurship opportunities.
  • ✓Practice financial management.
  • ✓Promote environmental conservation.
  • ✓Maintain healthy lifestyles.
  • ✓Be prepared for disasters and emergencies.
  • ✓Participate actively in community development.
