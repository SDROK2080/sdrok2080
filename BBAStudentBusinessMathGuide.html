🎯 SMART Goal: Master linear functions, quadratic equations, financial math & differentiation for economic analysis within one semester.
UNIT 1 Straight Lines & Functions

Linear functions, demand/supply, elasticity, least squares, Excel basics

📖 Free PDF: Business Math Handbook 📖 Free PDF: Math of Economics & Business ▶️ Khan Academy: Microeconomics ▶️ Khan: Linear Equations
💡 Student Tip: Practice plotting demand & supply curves in Excel. Use the formula y = mx + c to model price-quantity relationships.
UNIT 2 Simultaneous Equations

Equilibrium, break-even, consumer/producer surplus, IS-LM model

📖 Free PDF: Business Math Handbook (Ch. 4-5) ▶️ Khan Academy: Supply & Demand
💡 Student Tip: Use Excel's Solver tool to find equilibrium points where Qd = Qs. Draw graphs to visualize surplus areas.
UNIT 3 Quadratic Equations

Graphs, solving quadratics, economic applications (profit, cost curves)

📖 Free PDF: Intro to Math Economics (Ch. 2) ▶️ Khan Academy: Quadratic Functions
💡 Student Tip: Remember: x = [-b ± √(b²-4ac)] / 2a. Use Excel to plot parabolas and find break-even points where TR = TC.
UNIT 4 Non-Linear Functions & Numerical Methods

Cubic, exponential, logarithmic, hyperbolic functions; Bisection & Newton-Raphson methods

📖 Free PDF: Intro to Math Economics (Ch. 3-4) ▶️ Khan Academy: Exponential & Log Functions
💡 Student Tip: Newton-Raphson formula: x₁ = x₀ - f(x₀)/f'(x₀). Practice with a calculator first, then implement in Excel.
UNIT 5 Financial Mathematics

Sequences & series, simple/compound interest, depreciation, NPV, IRR, annuities, bonds

📖 Free PDF: Business Math Handbook (Ch. 8-15) 📖 Free PDF: Math of Economics & Business (Ch. 3) ▶️ Khan Academy: Finance & Capital Markets
💡 Student Tip: Master the compound interest formula: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt). Use Excel's PV(), FV(), and NPV() functions for quick calculations.
UNIT 6 Differentiation & Applications

Slope, rules of differentiation, marginal analysis, optimization, elasticity

📖 Free PDF: Intro to Math Economics (Ch. 5) ▶️ Tyler Wallace: Business Calculus (9 hrs)
💡 Student Tip: Marginal Cost = dC/dQ. To maximize profit, set MR = MC (i.e., dR/dQ = dC/dQ). Use second derivative test to confirm max/min.
🎯 SMART Goal: Apply integration, linear programming, matrix algebra, partial differentiation, differential & difference equations to real business problems.
UNIT 1 Integration & Applications

Standard forms, substitution, parts, definite integrals, consumer/producer surplus, area under curves

📖 Free PDF: Intro to Math Economics (Ch. 6) ▶️ Tyler Wallace: Integration (Business Calculus)
💡 Student Tip: Consumer Surplus = ∫(Demand) dQ - P×Q. Producer Surplus = P×Q - ∫(Supply) dQ. Sketch the area first before integrating!
UNIT 2 Linear Programming

Inequalities, formulation, graphical method, simplex method (2 variables), duality

📖 Free PDF: Intro to Math Economics (Ch. 7) ▶️ Playlist: Simplex Method (Joshua) ▶️ Jonah Emmanuel: Simplex Method Explained ▶️ Kauserwise: Simplex Solved Problems
💡 Student Tip: Step 1: Convert inequalities to equations using slack variables. Step 2: Set up the simplex tableau. Step 3: Identify pivot column (most negative in Z row). Step 4: Identify pivot row (smallest ratio). Step 5: Row operations until no negatives in Z row.
UNIT 3 Linear Algebra & Applications

Matrices, determinants, inverse, Cramer's rule, Gauss elimination, Gauss-Jordan, input-output analysis, Hawkins-Simon

📖 Free PDF: Intro to Math Economics (Ch. 7) ▶️ Khan Academy: Matrices
💡 Student Tip: For a 2×2 matrix [a b; c d], determinant = ad - bc. Inverse = (1/det) × [d -b; -c a]. Hawkins-Simon: all principal minors must be positive for viability.
UNIT 4 Functions of Several Variables

Partial differentiation, elasticity, utility, production functions, unconstrained & constrained optimization, Lagrange multipliers

📖 Free PDF: Intro to Math Economics (Ch. 5) ▶️ Tyler Wallace: Partial Derivatives
💡 Student Tip: Lagrange method: Set up L = f(x,y) - λ[g(x,y) - c]. Solve ∂L/∂x = 0, ∂L/∂y = 0, ∂L/∂λ = 0 simultaneously. Great for budget constraint problems!
UNIT 5 First-Order Differential Equations

Order & degree, linear DE with constant coefficients, limited/unlimited growth, market price dynamics, exact DE

📖 Free PDF: Intro to Math Economics (Ch. 8) ▶️ Khan Academy: Differential Equations
💡 Student Tip: For dy/dt + ay = b, the solution is y(t) = (y₀ - b/a)e^(-at) + b/a. This models market adjustment toward equilibrium over time.
UNIT 6 Difference Equations & Dynamic Analysis

First-order difference equations, cobweb model, lagged Keynesian model, duopoly price adjustment

📖 Free PDF: Difference Equations in Economics 📖 Free PDF: Intro to Math Economics (Ch. 8)
💡 Student Tip: Cobweb model: P(t) depends on Qs(t) which depends on P(t-1). If |slope of supply| < |slope of demand|, the market converges to equilibrium. Draw the "cobweb" spiral on your graph!
