Day 1

Foundations & Digital-AI Literacy

Focus module: Module 1 – Digital & AI Literacy · Duration: 5.0 hrs
TimeSessionMethodDetails
08:45–09:15Registration & Opening CeremonyCeremonialWelcome remarks by municipal education officials and school heads; lighting of the lamp; introduction of facilitators and program objectives.
09:15–09:45Ice-Breaking & Team FormationGamesName games and a quick mixer activity; students are grouped into fixed training teams that will stay together through the community project.
09:45–10:15Pre-Training AssessmentWritten / Digital QuizBaseline questionnaire covering all 10 module areas to measure starting knowledge and attitudes.
10:15–10:30Tea Break—Short refreshment break.
10:30–11:30Computer Basics & Safe Internet UseHands-on LabModule 1
Operating a computer/smartphone confidently, file handling, and browsing safely; identifying phishing links and unsafe websites.
11:30–12:30Digital Citizenship & Online SafetyRole PlayModule 1
Small-group role plays on cyberbullying, privacy settings, and reporting online harm; group debrief on digital rights and responsibilities.
12:30–13:00Lunch Break—Midday meal and rest.
13:00–14:00AI Tools & Responsible AI UseLive Demo + DiscussionModule 1
Guided demonstration of common AI tools (chatbots, translators, image generators); facilitated discussion on accuracy, bias, and ethical use in schoolwork.
14:00–14:30Daily Reflection & JournalingIndividual ReflectionStudents record one thing learned, one question, and one way they will apply it — collected as part of continuous assessment.
Day 2

Communication & Leadership

Focus module: Module 2 – Communication & Leadership · Duration: 5.0 hrs
TimeSessionMethodDetails
08:45–09:00Attendance & EnergizerWarm-up GameVoice and confidence warm-up exercise (tongue twisters, mirror-speaking pairs).
09:00–10:15Public Speaking FundamentalsInteractive Lecture + PracticeModule 2
Structuring a short speech, managing stage fear, using voice and body language; each student delivers a 1-minute impromptu talk.
10:15–10:30Tea Break—Short refreshment break.
10:30–11:45Teamwork & Group DynamicsTeam-Building ActivityModule 2
Rope/human-knot and tower-building challenges that require cooperation; facilitator-led debrief connecting the activity to real teamwork skills.
11:45–12:30Lunch Break—Midday meal and rest.
12:30–13:30Leadership Styles & QualitiesCase StudiesModule 2
Discussion of local and national youth leaders as case studies; students identify leadership traits they want to build.
13:30–14:15Presentation Skills WorkshopPractical AssignmentModule 2
Groups prepare a 2-minute presentation on an assigned topic using simple visual aids; peer feedback using a scoring rubric.
14:15–14:30Daily ReflectionIndividual ReflectionJournal entry and quick group share-out on confidence gained.
Day 3

Critical Thinking & Problem Solving

Focus module: Module 3 – Critical Thinking & Problem Solving · Duration: 5.0 hrs
TimeSessionMethodDetails
08:45–09:00Attendance & EnergizerLogic PuzzleQuick riddle or lateral-thinking puzzle solved in pairs.
09:00–10:15Logical Reasoning & Analytical ThinkingPuzzles + Guided PracticeModule 3
Pattern recognition, cause-and-effect reasoning, and basic argument evaluation using age-appropriate brain teasers.
10:15–10:30Tea Break—Short refreshment break.
10:30–11:45Introduction to Project-Based LearningInteractive LectureModule 3
What a good project question looks like; how to break a problem into research, design, and action steps.
11:45–12:30Lunch Break—Midday meal and rest.
12:30–13:45Innovation & Design ThinkingIdeation WorkshopModule 3
Groups pick a real school or community problem and sketch a simple, low-cost solution using a design-thinking worksheet.
13:45–14:15Mini Case-Study ChallengeGroup Problem-SolvingModule 3
Timed challenge applying reasoning skills to a short scenario; each group presents its solution in two minutes.
14:15–14:30Daily ReflectionIndividual ReflectionJournal entry on a problem-solving approach they will reuse.
Day 4

Career Guidance & Financial Literacy

Focus modules: Module 4 – Career Guidance & Module 5 – Financial Literacy · Duration: 5.0 hrs
TimeSessionMethodDetails
08:45–09:00Attendance & EnergizerWarm-up DiscussionQuick pair-share: "What did you want to be at age 8?"
09:00–10:00Career Planning & Self-AssessmentInterest InventoryModule 4
Students complete a simple interests-and-strengths worksheet and map it to possible career clusters.
10:00–10:15Entrepreneurship & Vocational OpportunitiesGuest TalkModule 4
Short talk from a local entrepreneur or vocational trainer on real pathways available in the municipality.
10:15–10:30Tea Break—Short refreshment break.
10:30–11:15Entrepreneurship Q&A and Idea PitchGuided DiscussionModule 4
Students ask the guest speaker questions and sketch one small business or service idea for their community.
11:15–12:00Lunch Break—Midday meal and rest.
12:00–13:00Budgeting & SavingPractical ExerciseModule 5
Students build a simple monthly budget from a sample allowance, practicing needs-vs-wants decisions and saving targets.
13:00–14:15Banking & Digital PaymentsDemo / Bank VisitModule 5
Walkthrough of opening a savings account and using mobile/digital payment apps safely, ideally with a local bank representative.
14:15–14:30Daily ReflectionIndividual ReflectionJournal entry on one career step and one saving habit to start this month.
Day 5

Environment & Well-being

Focus modules: Module 6 – Environmental & Climate Education & Module 7 – Health & Well-being · Duration: 5.0 hrs
TimeSessionMethodDetails
08:45–09:00Attendance & EnergizerMovement GameQuick outdoor stretch and breathing activity.
09:00–10:00Waste Management & the 3RsHands-on Sorting ActivityModule 6
Students sort sample waste into reduce/reuse/recycle categories and discuss school and community waste practices.
10:00–10:15Biodiversity & Climate Adaptation (intro)Short TalkModule 6
Brief framing on local biodiversity and visible climate changes in the municipality.
10:15–10:30Tea Break—Short refreshment break.
10:30–11:30Nature Walk / Field ObservationField VisitModule 6
Guided walk around the school or community area to observe local plants, water sources, and signs of environmental stress; students note observations.
11:30–12:15Lunch Break—Midday meal and rest.
12:15–13:15Mental Health & Stress ManagementGuided Discussion + MindfulnessModule 7
Age-appropriate discussion on stress, emotions, and healthy coping strategies; a short guided breathing/mindfulness exercise.
13:15–14:15Nutrition & Reproductive HealthHealth-Worker Talk + Q&AModule 7
Age-appropriate session led by a health professional on nutrition, puberty, and reproductive health, with a confidential question box.
14:15–14:30Daily ReflectionIndividual ReflectionJournal entry on one environmental action and one well-being habit to adopt.
Day 6

Safety & Citizenship

Focus modules: Module 8 – Disaster Risk Reduction & Module 9 – Civic Education · Duration: 5.0 hrs
TimeSessionMethodDetails
08:45–09:00Attendance & EnergizerQuick Drill Warm-upPractice "duck-cover-hold" positioning as a warm-up.
09:00–10:15Earthquake & Fire PreparednessSimulation DrillModule 8
Facilitated by the local disaster management office: evacuation routes, safe zones, and a live earthquake/fire drill.
10:15–10:30Tea Break—Short refreshment break.
10:30–11:45Flood Preparedness & First AidPractical DemonstrationModule 8
Flood warning signs and safe response steps; hands-on first-aid practice (bandaging, recovery position, basic CPR awareness) with police/health support.
11:45–12:30Lunch Break—Midday meal and rest.
12:30–13:30Constitution & Local GovernanceQuiz + DiscussionModule 9
Key features of the constitution and the structure/role of the local municipal government, reinforced with a quiz game.
13:30–14:15Citizenship & the SDGsGroup DiscussionModule 9
Students match Sustainable Development Goals to local issues they have observed and discuss their role as active citizens.
14:15–14:30Daily ReflectionIndividual ReflectionJournal entry on one civic action and one safety step to share with family.
Day 7

Community Project & Closing

Focus module: Module 10 – Community Project, Post-Training Assessment & Closing Ceremony · Duration: 5.5 hrs
TimeSessionMethodDetails
08:45–09:00Attendance & EnergizerTeam ChantTeams reconvene with a short energizer to mark the final day.
09:00–09:45Community Project PlanningGroup PlanningModule 10
Each fixed training team selects one local issue (from earlier modules) and drafts a one-page action plan.
09:45–11:00Community Survey / Field VisitField Data CollectionModule 10
Teams go into the community (or interview visiting community members) to gather quick data or opinions relevant to their chosen issue.
11:00–11:15Tea Break—Short refreshment break.
11:15–12:15Project Development & Report WritingGroup WorkModule 10
Teams compile findings into a short report/poster and prepare a 5-minute presentation.
12:15–13:00Lunch Break—Midday meal and rest.
13:00–13:45Community Project PresentationsPanel EvaluationModule 10
Each team presents to a panel of trainers, teachers, and municipal representatives using the community project evaluation rubric.
13:45–14:05Post-Training AssessmentWritten / Digital QuizSame-format questionnaire as Day 1's baseline, to measure knowledge and skill gained across all 10 modules.
14:05–14:30Closing Ceremony & Certificate DistributionCeremonialClosing remarks, feedback collection, group photo, and distribution of Certificates of Completion to students meeting attendance and performance criteria.
This session-wise schedule operationalizes the ISCDTM–2026 guide into a ready-to-run, day-by-day plan. Facilitators may compress it to 5 days by combining Days 2–3 and Days 5–6, or extend discussion time where local context calls for it, as long as each module's core session is preserved.
