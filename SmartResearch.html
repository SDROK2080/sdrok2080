SMART Research Framework

A Strategic Toolkit for Scholars, Researchers & Investigators

🎯

S — Specific

Define precise research questions. Narrow scope with clear variables, population, and context.

Example:

"Examine the effect of flipped classroom on Grade 11 math scores in Taulihawa district."

📏

M — Measurable

Establish quantifiable indicators. Use validated instruments, rubrics, and statistical benchmarks.

Tools:

Pre/post tests, Likert scales, GPA tracking, effect size (Cohen's d)

✅

A — Achievable

Align with available resources, institutional support, and your expertise timeline.

Checklist:

✓ Sample access ✓ Ethics approval ✓ Software/tools ✓ Supervisor support

🔗

R — Relevant

Connect to academic gaps, national education policy, or community needs in Nepal.

Relevance Lens:

NEB curriculum gaps, STEM education policy, local classroom realities

⏱

T — Time-bound

Set clear milestones: literature review, data collection, analysis, writing, and submission deadlines.

Typical Timeline:

Months 1-2: Review | 3-5: Data | 6-7: Analysis | 8-10: Write | 11-12: Submit

📋 Research Lifecycle Checklist

🔍
1. Initial Planning

Define problem, objectives, hypotheses, and conduct literature review.

 🎨
2. Research Design

Choose type (descriptive/experimental), variables, setting, and timeframe.

 📊
3. Methodology & Sampling

Qualitative/quantitative/mixed. Probability or non-probability sampling.

 🧹
4. Data Preparation

Clean, organize, code, and structure data for analysis.

 📈
5. Data Analysis

Apply statistical tests, thematic analysis, or computational models.

 📝
6. Reporting & Ethics

Ensure validity, reliability, ethical compliance, and publish findings.
